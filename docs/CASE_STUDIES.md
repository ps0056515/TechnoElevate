## Case Study 1 — Reducing Bench Idle Cost by 34%

**Client Profile**  
Mid-size IT staffing firm, 120 consultants, specialising in Java and cloud engineering talent. Offices in Hyderabad and Bengaluru.

**The Problem**

The firm was carrying an average of 18–22 consultants on the bench at any given time. Bench time was tracked in a shared Excel spreadsheet updated manually by three delivery managers. Because there was no centralised view, individual managers often didn't know which consultants other teams were trying to place — leading to duplicate outreach, missed submission windows, and consultants sitting idle for 3–5 weeks before anyone noticed.

Key pain points:
- No visibility into bench idle hour accumulation week-over-week
- Attention items (e.g. "Riya has been idle 21 days") buried in email threads
- Bench cost estimated at ₹28L/month but actual figure unknown
- Requirements pipeline managed separately; talent-to-requirement matching was manual and slow

**The Solution**

The firm deployed TechnoElevate and migrated all talent records, requirements, and client data in one week. The following modules had immediate impact:

- **Talent Lifecycle Dashboard** — delivery managers could see at a glance how many people were at each stage and who had been on the bench the longest
- **Bench Idle Chart** — the 4-week trend chart exposed the pattern of idle hours building up mid-month, which correlated with slow submission periods
- **Attention Engine** — automatically surfaced consultants with high idle hours and requirements stalled in sourcing, replacing the manual email flag system
- **Pipeline Kanban** — requirements were now visible to all delivery managers, enabling cross-team matching

**Results** (measured over 3 months post-deployment)

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Average bench idle days per consultant | 19 days | 11 days | **−42%** |
| Weekly bench idle hours (firm-wide) | 310 hrs | 204 hrs | **−34%** |
| Time from bench start to first submission | 8.2 days | 3.7 days | **−55%** |
| Attention items resolved same-day | ~30% | ~78% | **+48 pts** |
| Bench cost (monthly estimate) | ₹28L | ₹18.5L | **−34%** |

**Key Insight**

> "Before TechnoElevate, I'd find out a consultant had been idle for three weeks only when they emailed me asking for work. Now I see it in the dashboard before they even feel the delay."  
> — *Delivery Head*

## Case Study 2 — Preventing Contract Revenue Leakage

**Client Profile**  
Professional services firm managing 40+ active SOWs across 12 enterprise clients. Annual contracted revenue: $4.2M.

**The Problem**

Contract renewals were tracked in a master Excel file. The file was updated inconsistently, and with 40+ contracts the account management team frequently missed renewal windows. In one quarter, two contracts totalling $380,000 expired before renewal discussions were initiated — the clients had moved on to competitors.

Additional issues:
- Invoice overdue tracking was done in a separate spreadsheet, meaning overdue invoices weren't always visible to delivery managers
- No single view of utilisation across all contracts, making it hard to identify underutilised engagements that were at risk of client cancellation
- Account managers spent ~4 hours per week compiling contract status reports for leadership

**The Solution**

The firm's operations lead deployed TechnoElevate with the Contracts & SOWs module as the primary focus. All 43 active contracts were imported in a single afternoon using the Admin panel's bulk input flow.

Immediately, the Contracts panel showed:
- 6 contracts in **Expiring Soon** status (within 30 days)
- 3 contracts with **Invoice Overdue** flags
- 2 contracts at under 40% utilisation — at risk of scope reduction

The Attention Engine surfaced the 6 expiring contracts as HIGH-priority attention items, visible to the entire leadership team every morning.

**Results** (over 6 months post-deployment)

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Contracts that expired before renewal discussion | 2/quarter | 0/quarter | **−100%** |
| Average days from expiry to renewal notice | 4 days after | 28 days before | **+32 days lead time** |
| Overdue invoice discovery time | ~2 weeks | Same day | **−14 days** |
| Weekly contract reporting time (ops team) | 4 hrs/week | 20 min/week | **−87%** |
| Revenue protected from missed renewals | — | $620,000 (Year 1) | |

**Key Insight**

> "The Contracts panel paid for itself in the first month. We caught a $220K contract that was 12 days from expiry with no renewal conversation started. We got it renewed. That alone covered our entire year."  
> — *VP of Account Management*

## Case Study 3 — Scaling Managed Services Delivery Visibility

**Client Profile**  
Managed services provider running 14 concurrent client projects, team of 55 consultants. Mix of data engineering, DevOps, and application development engagements.

**The Problem**

Project status was communicated through weekly email updates and a PowerPoint deck circulated on Fridays. Leadership had no real-time view of project health. When a project became blocked, it often took 4–7 days for the blocker to reach a decision-maker who could act.

The engagement onboarding process was also inconsistent. Without a standardised checklist, some consultants started client engagements without completing background checks or having client system access provisioned — creating compliance risks and Day 1 productivity losses.

The firm was also growing: adding 3–4 new projects per quarter, which meant the existing spreadsheet-based tracking system was becoming unmanageable.

**The Solution**

TechnoElevate was deployed across the delivery organisation with a focus on three modules:

1. **Managed Services Projects** — all 14 active projects imported with current health status and blocking issues. The delivery leadership team now opened TechnoElevate as their first action each morning.

2. **Engagement Checklist** — a standard 7-stage checklist was created for all new engagements. Delivery managers were responsible for marking items complete; team leads could see overdue items in real time.

3. **Attention Engine** — blocked projects automatically generated HIGH-priority attention items, visible to the full leadership team within minutes rather than days.

**Workflow change**

Before: Project Manager emails a status update → Delivery Head reads it next morning → decides to escalate → 2-day resolution lag.

After: Project blocked → Attention item created (HIGH) → Delivery Head sees it immediately → one-click action → resolved same day.

**Results** (over 4 months post-deployment)

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Average time to resolve a blocked project | 5.2 days | 1.1 days | **−79%** |
| Projects reaching "blocked" status that stayed blocked > 3 days | 60% | 8% | **−52 pts** |
| Engagement checklist completion rate (Day 1 items) | 61% | 94% | **+33 pts** |
| Weekly leadership reporting time | 6 hrs (deck prep) | 0 hrs | **−100%** |
| Consultant Day 1 productivity issues | ~4/month | ~0.5/month | **−87%** |

**Key Insight**

> "Our Friday status deck was basically a history lesson. By the time leadership read it, things had already changed. TechnoElevate gives us today's picture, not last week's."  
> — *Head of Delivery*

**Scalability note**

When the firm onboarded 3 new projects in a single month, adding them to TechnoElevate took under 10 minutes per project. The dashboard instantly reflected the new projects in health metrics, utilisation data, and the attention engine — no spreadsheet updates, no new slides.
